# Robotic Palletising Systems - Integration & Testing

*Integrated 7 robotic systems for 8,000 SKUs, increasing warehouse throughput by 18%*

---

## THE PROBLEM (WHY)

**Market Context:**
E-commerce boom forcing logistics companies to automate warehouses rapidly. Vanderlande customer needed to triple outbound capacity without expanding physical footprint.

**User Pain Points:**
- Manual palletising bottleneck limiting order fulfillment capacity
- 8,000 different SKUs requiring flexible automation solution
- Supply chain disruptions delaying equipment delivery by 6+ weeks
- Tight timeline: holiday season deadlines non-negotiable

**The Opportunity:**
- €10M+ project automating warehouse outbound operations
- First deployment of APAL robotic palletisers in this configuration
- Success opens door to 3 additional distribution centers across Europe
- Demonstrate ability to integrate complex systems under pressure

**Your Unique Insight:**
> "System integration success isn't about perfect equipment - it's about systematic testing that catches edge cases before production launch."

---

## THE SOLUTION (WHAT)

**Vision:** Fully automated palletising line handling 8,000+ SKU variations with zero human intervention.

**Key Features:**
1. **Robotic Palletising:** 7 APAL units with adaptive gripping for diverse package sizes
2. **Pallet Wrapping Integration:** Automated stretch wrapping post-palletising
3. **SKU Recognition:** Vision systems identifying package dimensions and weight for optimal stacking
4. **System Integration:** Connected to WMS (Warehouse Management System) for real-time order processing

**Target Users:** Warehouse operations team, fulfillment center managers, logistics planners

**Differentiation:** First multi-robot APAL deployment for this SKU complexity, delivered ahead of schedule despite supply chain issues

---

## THE EXECUTION (HOW - STAR)

### SITUATION
Large logistics customer expanding warehouse automation capabilities. 12 robotic palletising systems ordered, but supply chain disruptions delayed delivery. Holiday season deadlines approaching - late delivery would cost customer €500K+ in lost capacity.

### TASK
Integrate and commission 7 of 12 robotic palletising systems in 7 months (vs. standard 8-month timeline). Owned technical integration, testing protocols, and production validation.

### ACTION

**Discovery:**
- Mapped entire outbound flow: WMS → conveyor → robot → wrapping → shipping
- Identified 15 integration points requiring custom programming
- Created risk matrix for supply chain delays and mitigation plans

**Strategy:**
- Prioritized 7 highest-throughput lines first (80/20 rule - covers 70% of volume)
- Designed parallel commissioning approach instead of sequential
- Created standardized testing protocol reusable across all 7 systems

**Execution:**
- Integrated PLCs, safety systems, and WMS interfaces for 7 robotic cells
- Developed systematic testing phases: Dry-Run → Happy Flow → Production Flow
- Programmed custom logic for 8,000 SKU variations (package dimensions, weights, stacking patterns)
- Coordinated with 3 subcontractors (robotics, conveyors, wrapping equipment)
- Validated system performance under real production loads

**Challenges:**
- **Supply Chain Delays:** Reprioritized commissioning sequence, worked overtime to compress timeline
- **SKU Complexity:** Built simulation environment to test edge cases before production
- **Cross-Vendor Integration:** Established weekly syncs, created shared issue tracking system

### RESULT

**Metrics:**
- **Timeline Performance:** Delivered 7 systems in 7 months (1 month ahead of adjusted schedule)
- **Throughput Increase:** +18% outbound capacity through faster, more consistent palletising
- **Quality:** Zero safety incidents during commissioning and first 3 months of production
- **Reusability:** Testing protocols used for remaining 5 systems with 30% faster commissioning

**Impact:**
- Customer met holiday season capacity requirements (avoided €500K+ revenue loss)
- Vanderlande secured 3 follow-on projects at other customer sites
- Personal contribution recognized with project excellence award

**Learning:**
- Standardization scales: reusable testing protocols saved weeks on subsequent systems
- Under pressure, communication frequency matters more than meeting duration
- Early simulation of edge cases prevents costly production failures
